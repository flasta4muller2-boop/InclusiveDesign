Why it matters

Built environments perform through human experience.

Inclusive design helps project teams study how people use, interpret, navigate, wait, work, recover, and participate in a place. The value shows up in design quality, operational fit, dignity, safety, comfort, and clearer project decisions.

01

Usability

Spaces are assessed through everyday actions, decision points, transitions, and support needs.

What this means: user friction can be addressed before it becomes a field issue.
02

Dignity

Design choices are reviewed for privacy, choice, independence, and the ability to ask for help without stigma.

What this means: support spaces can feel integrated, legible, and respectful.
03

Orientation

Wayfinding, lighting, acoustics, sightlines, landmarks, and thresholds are considered together.

What this means: people spend less effort decoding the environment.
04

Outcomes

Recommendations are framed as practical decisions across function, aesthetics, operations, and budget.

What this means: the team gets usable input rather than a separate checklist.

Entry points

Three natural moments to bring inclusive design into the work.

The model is intentionally lightweight. Involvement scales to the project phase, the project type, and the decision the team needs to make.

Pursuit

Define design intent in human terms before the story is fixed.

At pursuit stage, inclusive design can help a team connect project understanding to how people will actually arrive, wait, orient, work, receive support, and use the environment.

Review RFPs and RFQs for inclusive design relevance

Identify where passenger, resident, worker, visitor, caregiver, or staff experience could strengthen the response.

Draft proposal and SOQ language

Translate human-centered design intent into clear language that can support differentiation without overstating the role.

Support shortlist interviews and presentations

Prepare talking points that help the team answer questions with credibility, specificity, and a clear project lens.

Develop preliminary scoping recommendations

Recommend whether inclusive design input should continue into the project and where it could be most useful.

Questions teams usually ask

Designed to be useful, bounded, and easy to start.

We already consider accessibility. How is this different?

Good. This builds on that foundation and adds a more structured way to evaluate how people actually experience and use a space.

Does this apply to my project type?

Yes, when people use, navigate, interpret, wait, work, recover, or interact with the environment. The level of support should match the project.

Is there a cost to the project?

Initial engagement can begin within existing workflows. Some projects may later choose to scope additional support if deeper involvement adds value.

Does this work remotely?

Yes. Most pursuit and design development support is document-based. Post-occupancy evaluation usually requires a site visit.
